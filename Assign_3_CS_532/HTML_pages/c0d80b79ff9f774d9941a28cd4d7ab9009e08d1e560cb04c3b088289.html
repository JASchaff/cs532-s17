Public university students react to Betsy DeVos' confirmation as Secretary of Education

By Mitchell Wellman, University of Virginia 3:09 pm EST February 7, 2017

3.1k shares
SHARE
TWEET
SMS
SEND
EMAIL

The new Secretary of Education, Betsy DeVos. (File photo: Erik Lesser, EPA)

Today was another first in U.S. history. Betsy DeVos was confirmed as Secretary of Education thanks to a tie-breaker vote by Vice President Mike Pence — the first time a U.S. vice president has broken a tie to confirm a presidential Cabinet candidate. (The VPOTUS is the president of the Senate. You knew that, right?)

Related: 6 things college students need to know about Betsy DeVos, Trump’s pick for Education Secretary

Politicians and commentators with a range of opinions have been speaking out on Twitter since the news of the confirmation broke, expressing general concerns about the future public education in general and about specific issues like the treatment of LGBTQ youth in schools.

The Senate has confirmed Betsy DeVos as the nation's 11th Education secretary in a historic vote: https://t.co/uP9a6xZqRL pic.twitter.com/LHHy9nhvrJ

— USA TODAY (@USATODAY) February 7, 2017

President Trump maintained DeVos would serve as a necessary change-agent, working against “the failed status quo.”

Senate Dems protest to keep the failed status quo. Betsy DeVos is a reformer, and she is going to be a great Education Sec. for our kids!

— President Trump (@POTUS) February 7, 2017

Conservative political pundit Charlie Kirk noted the positive effect DeVos will have on socioeconomically disadvantaged children. Kirk is 21 years old and attended Wheeling High School, a public high school in Wheeling, Illinois, according to his LinkedIn profile.

With DeVos being confirmed millions of poor kids and families will have their lives changed. Ending the cycle of poverty! #SchoolChoice

— Charlie Kirk (@charliekirk11) February 7, 2017

But many public school advocates oppose DeVos — including current students. Numerous public university students shared success stories of their experience in the public education system and concerns about the future of public ed under DeVos.

As someone who went to a "high risk" public school and am now at the top of my class at a public university I can say that she is mistaken https://t.co/ytKw0ouF3D

— Emily (@ESimpy) February 7, 2017

Thank God I went to public school before #devos; Sorry to my future students #DeVosvote #NoonDeVos

— 🗽Bad Hombre 🇺🇸 (@boypatino) February 7, 2017

so glad that I go to a public university!!! 🙃 https://t.co/QIBskSqZea

— katie (@katiethoele) February 7, 2017

man, i go to a public university IM SCREWED 💀

— harrison (@princepepi) February 7, 2017

If I have to go to class at my public university soon with Betsy Devos as the new education secretary I will die

— Valerie Higgins (@FourForYouVal) February 7, 2017

love that in my first year at a public university Betsy DeVos is going to be secretary of education!!! Gonna be fun!!! Whoooo!!!

— kari (@kari_merrill98) February 7, 2017

I just want to appreciate my public university today because DeVos will burn it down tomorrow.

— Anu Vora (@entreprANU) February 7, 2017

No coincidence the moment #devos was confirmed the sky unleashed a torrential downpour on the campus of a public university. #goodbyeedu pic.twitter.com/VdH5CkuUvl

— Kate Sonka (@kate_sonka) February 7, 2017

As someone who went to private school and now goes to a public university I oppose the confirmation of Betsy DeVos, this is a huge setback.

— Francesco Corso❄ (@xenofrancesco) February 7, 2017

I went to a public school and I'm doing pretty well in college @BetsyDeVos fight me pic.twitter.com/DmWl8Xrctx

— jules (@Julster11) February 7, 2017

Serious question: now that VP Pence has forced DeVos through the Senate, will students be held accountable for plagiarism? DeVos wasn't.

— Kaivan Shroff (@KaivanShroff) February 7, 2017

On the other hand, maybe this is a moment when more focus will be put on improving public university systems.

Everyone is complaing about @BetsyDeVos, and I'm just sitting here in an already failing public university. @ArizonaState

— Chad (@Wazzlejazzlebof) February 7, 2017

Related: 5 ways to show your public school pride

One specific point of contention was DeVos’ views on policies surrounding LGBTQ youth in schools. National organizations such as Lambda Legal spoke out against the confirmation. DeVos has been criticized by various pro-LGBTQ groups for her funding of anti-LGBT conversion therapy and anti-gay marriage movements.

We're already in court for #transgender students & are ready to file more cases if we must. Take note, @BetsyDeVos. #DeVos #WeObject

— Lambda Legal (@LambdaLegal) February 7, 2017

All students includes students with disabilities, students of color, LGBT+ students… She knows this, right? https://t.co/UXCJbvL75P

— Ash B (@bodyposibalance) February 7, 2017

DeVos herself tweeted about her victory in good spirits, saying she aims to help “all US students.”

I appreciate the Senate's diligence & am honored to serve as @usedgov Secretary. Let's improve options & outcomes for all US students.

— Betsy DeVos (@BetsyDeVos) February 7, 2017

Mitchell Wellman is a student at the University of Virginia and a USA TODAY College digital producer.
